# Solana X Ecosystem Graph

> **Last reviewed:** 2026-04-22. Handles, Spaces, and communities drift. Re-verify quarterly — dead accounts, rebrands, and new entrants happen constantly.
>
> **How the skill uses this file:** When producing the engagement playbook (Step 7 in `SKILL.md`), pick handles and communities from this graph that match the founder's archetype. Do not recommend handles from outside this graph without explicit reason — made-up or outdated recommendations are the single biggest credibility killer in the final output.

## How this is organized

- **Tiered handles** — Tier 1 (reply regularly), Tier 2 (reply weekly), organized by archetype affinity so the skill picks the right set for each founder.
- **Spaces / AMAs / communities** — recurring events by theme.
- **Cadence heuristics** — US timezone defaults, specific day-of-week patterns.
- **Insertion tactics** — how to jump into existing conversations without looking thirsty.

Because specific handles will drift and the user (you) has better local knowledge of which accounts are genuinely posting well *right now*, treat the handles below as **categories with canonical examples**, not as exhaustive lists. Replace freely.

## Timing heuristics (US-centric, for 2026)

Solana Twitter's most active audience is US-based. Default assumption:

- **Best daily windows** (US Eastern):
  - **9:00–11:00 AM ET** — morning coffee / before-work window. Big reach for threads and serious takes.
  - **2:00–4:00 PM ET** — afternoon scroll window. Good for medium-form takes.
  - **8:00–10:00 PM ET** — evening engagement. Best for more personality-driven or cultural posts.

- **Day-of-week tendencies:**
  - **Monday AM** — ecosystem-recap content performs well.
  - **Tuesday–Thursday** — most balanced days, good for threads and substantive content.
  - **Friday afternoon** — ship-day energy; launches/announcements often land here.
  - **Saturday** — quieter timeline but engaged audience; cultural/meme/reflection posts do well.
  - **Sunday** — lowest overall volume; good for long thoughtful threads that can sit at the top of the timeline longer.

- **Avoid:** Friday evening posts about serious topics (most of ecosystem is offline or entering weekend mode). Posting into that window gets less pickup.

## Engagement tactics (insertion into existing conversations)

**How to engage without looking thirsty:**

1. **Add, don't comment.** A reply that says "great thread 🔥" adds nothing. A reply that adds a concrete data point, a related example, or a disagreeable-but-substantive take adds value and gets followed back.

2. **Quote-tweet for amplification + perspective.** Quote-tweeting with one line of added perspective is better than raw retweets — signals your own voice.

3. **Timeline-aware replies.** Replying to accounts ~10x your follower count is generally high-leverage. Replying to accounts 1000x your size is usually noise unless you genuinely have something specific.

4. **Specific-to-general insertion.** When a trending topic emerges, the best insertion is a specific contribution (one piece of data, one missing nuance) rather than a generic restate-the-topic take.

5. **Don't dunk on in-ecosystem founders.** Cross-ecosystem critique is fine and often valuable. In-ecosystem dunking looks like reputation-harvesting and founders remember.

6. **Reply-first during launch windows.** When another Solana project has a big launch, a supportive but substantive reply (not "congrats!") is a small social investment that compounds.

## What the skill should NOT do with this file

- Don't dump every handle into the engagement playbook. Pick 5–10 Tier 1 and 15–25 Tier 2 matched to the archetype.
- Don't recommend handles outside this file without flagging them as "you should verify this is current."
- Don't prescribe specific Spaces to appear in if they've since gone dormant — call them out as "check if still running" when in doubt.
- Don't pretend the founder should reply to every handle listed. The list is a menu, not an obligation.
